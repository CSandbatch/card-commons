# Governance

Card Commons begins as a maintainer-led open specification.

## Roles

- **Maintainers** merge changes, cut releases, and resolve process disputes.
- **Editors** improve a named specification area and review consistency.
- **Contributors** propose research, examples, corrections, or implementations.

## Decisions

Routine editorial fixes use pull-request review. Material protocol, scope, or
governance changes require a public issue, an explicit decision record, and
maintainer approval. Compatibility and dissenting alternatives must be
recorded.

## Releases

Protocol artifacts use semantic versions. During `0.x`, minor releases may
break compatibility when accompanied by migration notes. A `1.0` release
requires at least two independent prototypes that exchange and render the same
card documents successfully.

## Conflicts of interest

Decision participants disclose financial or organizational interests relevant
to a proposal. Governance may evolve toward a multi-stakeholder model after
independent implementations and an active contributor community exist.
